# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Tech-savvy founders, developers, and AI builders in the Israeli startup scene attending AI Israel Club events. People who are either already building solo or seriously considering it. They're builders themselves — they recognise patterns, spot BS fast, and respond to practitioners over consultants.

## Product Purpose

A 20-slide Reveal.js speaker deck by Ron Zaidman: "The Solo Founder Playbook in the AI Era." Covers Ron's founder journey (startup shutdown → dopamine.chat), the case for solo founding in the AI era, a practical playbook, the pitfall of isolation, GTM lessons, and his tech stack. The deck exists to inspire the audience to embrace solo founding, and build awareness of dopamine.chat and Solo Founders Hub.

## Brand Personality

Bold, practical, direct. The voice of a builder who has been through it — not a polished thought leader. Confident without being arrogant. Honest about failure. Zero filler.

## Anti-references

Generic AI-generated slides: flat gradient backgrounds, gradient text, hero-metric number cards, glassmorphism panels, stock-photo aesthetics, identical icon-heading-text card grids. Any deck that looks like it was produced in Canva in ten minutes.

## Design Principles

1. **Earned credibility, not performed authority.** Every visual choice should feel like it came from a practitioner, not a template. Specificity over polish — a concrete data point beats a decorative flourish.
2. **One idea per slide, zero cognitive tax.** This is a live talk. The audience has seconds per slide. Visual hierarchy must do the work; nothing should require reading time to find the point.
3. **Speed of comprehension is the primary UX metric.** Contrast, scale, and layout must guide the eye instantly to the most important element on each slide.
4. **Actively refuse the AI-slop aesthetic.** The anti-reference is the generic AI-generated deck. Every design decision should be deliberate and distinctive — not a training-data reflex.
5. **The terminal is the one dark surface.** The slide canvas is white and neutral. The only intentional dark surface is the terminal/code block, which signals "real agent output" through the surface shift — not aesthetic preference.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No specific WCAG target. Reduced motion is already handled via `prefers-reduced-motion` in the codebase.
